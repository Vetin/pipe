# Feature Contract Step

Define the product behavior in requirements and acceptance criteria before
architecture or code. Ask only blocking questions, make assumptions explicit,
and stop on security-critical or business-critical ambiguity.

Use the ambiguity taxonomy before drafting. Ask at most five high-impact
blocking questions, re-score after vague answers, and stop on unresolved
security, data-loss, public-contract, compliance, migration, or ownership risk.
For standard and comprehensive rigor, include at least one pre-mortem or
inversion risk and state whether existing behavior can be reused.
